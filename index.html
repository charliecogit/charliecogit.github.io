Fractions Are Easy!

Important steps to multiplying fractions:

  1. Simplify the fractions if possible.
  2. Multiply the numerators to get the new numerator.
  3. Multiply the denominators to get the new denominator
  4. Simplify the answer if possible

Examples:

`1/4 xx 2/8 = 1/4 xx 1/4 = 1/16`       Here `2/8` is simplified to `1/4`

If we do not simplify the fractions first, the numerator and denominator both become larger

`1/4 xx 2/8 = 2/32 = 1/16`       Simplifying `2/32 = 1/16` is not too difficult


But here is an example where it is helpful to simplify first

`25/75 xx 28/49 = (25*28)/(75*49) = 700/3675`       Try simplifying `700/3675` in your head!

Let's simplify the fractions first

`25/75 xx 28/49 = 1/3 xx 4/7 = 4/21`

Word Problem:

Frank's kitty ate `2/3` of a slice of the pizza shown below. What fraction of the pizza did the kitty ate?


Answer:

One slice of pizza is `1/8` of the entire pizza.

So `2/3` of a slice is `2/3 xx 1/8 = 2/24 =1/12` of the entire pizza.


Notice that `2/3 xx 1/8 = (2*1)/(3*8) = (2*1)/(8*3) = 2/8 xx 1/3 = 1/4 xx 1/3 = 1/12`

This observation can be useful in a problem like this: `25/49 xx 14/75 = 14/49 xx 25/75 = 2/7 xx 1/3 = 2/21`
